Location

Biševo is a picturesque island situated in the Adriatic Sea, off the coast of Croatia. This remote and enchanting destination, known for its stunning natural beauty and crystal-clear waters, is part of the Central Dalmatian archipelago. Located approximately 5 kilometres southwest of the larger island of Vis, Biševo is a hidden gem in the Adriatic, accessible only by boat.

History

“STONE BOOK” FROM THE AGE OF DINOSAURS

The collision of the Adriatic Carbonate Platform and Europe resulted in a tectonic rise that shaped the Dinaric mountains and these hills, present-day islands of the Vis archipelago. Due to this reason, spectacular cliffs can be found all along the coasts of these islands.

PAVAO MARTINIS - THE US KING OF SALMON

He was born on Biševo island in 1893, and through possessing enormous fishing experience gained in his homeland, he arrived in Astoria, Oregon, having heard that there were fishermen from Komiža in Tacoma. Pavao Martinis went down in history of American fishing not only because of the record catches of salmon in Bristol Bay and the Bering Sea, but also as the first American fisherman who discovered salmon in the rich and dangerous waters of the Aleutian Islands. In 1956, the US President Dwight D. Eisenhower awarded him the title King of Salmon for his fishing endeavors.

Community

In the past, Bisevo had a small population engaged in traditional activities such as fishing, agriculture, and livestock farming. These communities were often self-sustaining and relied on the resources of the island and the surrounding sea. Over time, factors like economic changes and urbanization led to a decline in the permanent population on the island. Many people moved to the nearby larger island of Vis or to the mainland for better economic opportunities.

Today, Bisevo's history and cultural heritage are still evident in its architecture, including old stone houses and structures, as well as in the stories and traditions passed down through generations. The island's historical sites and landmarks also reflect its past, making it an interesting destination for those interested in exploring the history of the region.

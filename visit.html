Transport

There are numerous ways to travel to Biševo if you're planning to stay in Vis, Komiža, or Split. Here are our favorites:

  1. Ferry from Vis Island: Most visitors to Biševo arrive on the neighbouring island of Vis, which is more accessible and has regular ferry services from Split on the mainland. From Vis, you can take a ferry or boat tour to Biševo. The ferry ride from Vis to Biševo is relatively short.

  2. Private Boat or Charter: Many tourists choose to hire a private boat or charter a boat tour from towns like Komiža on Vis or Split on the mainland. This option offers more flexibility in terms of departure times and allows you to explore Biševo at your own pace.

  3. Organised Boat Tours: Numerous boat tour companies in the region offer organised tours to Biševo and the famous Blue Cave. These tours often depart from various locations on the mainland and neighbouring islands. They provide a guided experience and typically include other stops at nearby attractions.

  4. Yacht or Sailboat: If you have access to a yacht or sailboat, you can sail to Biševo and anchor in one of the island's bays. This offers a unique and personalised way to explore the island.

  5. Kayak: For the more adventurous traveler, you can rent a kayak on Vis or another nearby island and paddle to Biševo. This option is suitable for experienced kayakers and can provide a unique perspective of the coastline.

Persons with disabilities or reduced mobility are ensured transportation by an electric vehicle from the port of Mezoporat to the visitor center. The visitor center is adapted to persons with disabilities. Furthermore, the information boards on the thematic educational trail are also available in Braille.

The Blue Cave

The Blue Cave, located on the enchanting island of Biševo in Croatia, is a natural wonder that captivates visitors with its mesmerizing beauty. This unique sea cave is renowned for its stunning azure-blue waters, which are illuminated by sunlight filtering through an underwater entrance. The resulting play of light creates an otherworldly, ethereal ambiance within the cave's interior.

The Blue Cave is at its most breathtaking during the morning hours when the sunlight hits the water at the perfect angle, creating an almost surreal, iridescent blue glow. Visitors can access the cave by a small boat, and once inside, they are treated to a serene and magical experience, making the Blue Cave a must-see destination for anyone exploring the Dalmatian coast.

The Monk Seal Cave

The Monk Seal Cave, also known as Medvidina Cave, on the island of Biševo in Croatia, is a place of natural wonder and historical significance. This coastal cave, like the Blue Cave on the same island, boasts crystal-clear waters that shimmer in shades of blue and green. However, what makes the Monk Seal Cave particularly intriguing is its connection to the Mediterranean monk seal, one of the world's most endangered marine mammals. Historically, these elusive creatures sought refuge and raised their pups in the cave's hidden corners.






While monk seals are now extremely rare in the area, the cave continues to be a symbol of the island's rich natural heritage and offers a captivating environment for exploration. Visitors can navigate its underwater depths with snorkeling or by taking a boat tour, immersing themselves in the history and natural beauty of Biševo.

History

THE MADONNA OF BIŠEVO - 13TH CENTURY

St Sylvester's Church

In the year 1050, a clergyman named Ivan, hailing from Split, oversaw the construction of a church on the plateau of Biševo, dedicated to Pope St. Sylvester (314 – 335). This historical figure was renowned for securing the right of Christians to openly profess their faith. Father Ivan entrusted the church to the Benedictines, who arrived from the Tremiti Islands. Initially built in the pre-Romanesque style, the church underwent architectural alterations in the subsequent centuries. During a turbulent era marked by battles between Venice and the Byzantine Empire for control over the region, the Monastery of St. Silvester on Biševo sought protection from Pope Alexander III. On May 2, 1181, the Pope issued a privilege that granted safeguard to this monastery. The document began with the Pope's address to the monastery's superior, Abbot Urso, and it was intended to extend protection to both the present and future monastic brethren.

GEO Trail

Cycle Path

The Geotrail on the enchanting island of Biševo, Croatia, is a captivating nature walk that showcases the island's geological wonders. The concept of educational-thematic trails that are used for the interpretation of the natural and cultural heritage, has both an educational and recreational character. The visitors may choose any segment of the educational trail, in line with their preferences, fitness and abilities.

Visitors can find a total of 25 information panels, presenting the most important locations of the island, and also present features of Biševo, such as its vegetation, the most important animal species, the speleological and geological phenomena and objects, historical buildings, the Roman well and artillery batteries, as well as thetraditional agriculture of the island.

With stunning coastal views, charming villages, and a relatively gentle terrain, this cycle path provides a delightful and active way to experience the island's enchanting landscapes and coastal charm.

Art Program

Bisevo Island Art Residency

The residency is focused on partnerships with other organizations and associations from the art, culture, ecology, biology and astronomy departments. The goal of their work is to create partnerships and initiatives that are focused on tangible results. The residency is a centre of exploration, creation and experimentality with the aim for cross-sector collaboration. The space is also acceptive for independent individuals who are initiating the projects in tune with the work of the residency.

THEIR GOAL
Creating a research centre, a residential space for collaborations and various initiatives, with a focus on experimentalism and cross-sector collaborations.

EXPECTED IMPACT / RESULTS
Create positive experiences through which both visitors and locals participate in life on the island and become part of its cultural landscape. The above established framework of activities will complement and build on each other encouraging a relationship of care and love towards the island of Biševo.
